About the Field Notes Journal

Published April 12, 2026 · A reader’s guide to the journal

The Field Notes Journal is where Field & Feather Co. gathers practical birdwatching guidance for beginners, growing birders, and curious lifelong naturalists. The goal is simple: publish clear, useful articles that help readers get outside more confidently, notice more, and enjoy birds with better context.

Starting point: Wherever you arrived from, use this guide to move into the next Field Notes Journal articles with a little more structure.

What you will find here

The journal is organized around the kinds of questions birders ask most often. Some readers arrive looking for a true beginner starting point. Others want help choosing binoculars, understanding migration, learning backyard habitat basics, or getting oriented to common birds in a particular state. The collection is designed so you can start with one article and keep following the trail into related topics.

How to use the collection

If you are new to birdwatching, begin with the foundational guides on getting started, bird identification, and gear. If you already bird regularly, jump into migration, regional guides, field-guide comparisons, or backyard habitat improvements. Each article is written to stand alone, but the internal links are there to help you build confidence topic by topic.

  • Begin with beginner guides if you want a strong foundation.
  • Use migration and identification articles when the field starts to feel more complex.
  • Use regional guides and gear coverage when you want more specificity.

Where to browse next
