# Subscriber Entry Page Strategy

Date: 2026-04-13
Owner: CEO
Purpose: identify the strongest journal entry pages for future lead magnet and subscriber placement once branded capture is activated.

## Executive summary
The best subscriber-entry pages are the pages where search intent, trust, and onward navigation already align. Based on current hub strength, internal-link density, content depth, and likely conversion posture, the top entry-page candidates cluster into four conversion buckets:
- beginner confidence
- gear buying confidence
- seasonal migration tracking
- backyard improvement / habit building

These are not all equal. Some pages are stronger for broad-volume subscriber capture, while others are better for narrower but higher-intent lead magnets.

## Placement guidance
Use the first branded subscriber placements in pages that already meet three conditions:
1. broad search intent or repeated seasonal demand
2. strong trust / educational value before the CTA appears
3. clear adjacent journey into deeper journal or product use

For first implementation, place subscriber/lead-magnet CTAs:
- after the main explanatory body and before FAQ on broad educational pages, or
- after conclusion on intent-specific pages where the reader has already received substantial value.

## What not to do
- Do not place one generic newsletter CTA on every page with identical copy.
- Do not lead with a subscriber ask too early on product-comparison pages.
- Do not activate capture before the offer matches the page intent.
- Do not weaken the premium editorial tone with noisy popups or generic growth hacks.

## Decision
For the first branded subscriber capture rollout, the highest-leverage entry pages are:
- Birdwatching for Beginners
- Essential Birding Gear for Beginners
- Backyard Birding for Beginners
- Spring Bird Migration
- How to Use eBird to Track Migration in Real Time

These should anchor the first subscriber-entry architecture, while regional pages become checklist-specific capture points and gear pages act as secondary trust/comparison surfaces.
