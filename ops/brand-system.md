# Field & Feather Co. Brand System

## Brand position
Field & Feather Co. sells refined birding tools for modern field journaling. The brand sits at the intersection of field-guide credibility, calm naturalist aesthetics, and practical observation utility.

## Promise
We help birders capture sightings, migration patterns, and life-list milestones with tools that feel beautiful enough to keep and useful enough to use repeatedly.

## Customer
Primary customer:
- Age: 35–65
- High-intent hobbyist birder or gift buyer for a serious birder
- Values quality, clarity, and species-specific relevance
- Comfortable with digital downloads and printable tools

Secondary customer:
- Casual birder entering the hobby
- Buys humorous or giftable products first, then migrates into structured journaling tools

## Brand attributes
- Observant
- Calm
- Field-guide literate
- Warm but not twee
- Practical
- Premium without luxury markup

## Voice and tone
Use:
- Precise, grounded language
- Naturalist vocabulary when helpful
- Short, calm sentences
- Quiet confidence
- Occasional poetic phrasing anchored in utility

Avoid:
- Excessive hype
- Overly cute copy
- Fake urgency
- Claims that imply scientific authority we do not hold
- Conservation claims without a real partner or donation mechanism

## Messaging pillars
1. Better field records
- Emphasize repeatable observation and useful note-taking.

2. Beautiful utility
- Products should feel elegant but work hard in practice.

3. Regional and species specificity
- Birders care deeply about where they bird and what they chase.

4. Low-friction ownership
- Instant delivery, easy printing, clear file formats, no clutter.

## Tagline options
Primary:
- Refined birding tools for modern field journaling.

Alternates:
- Every bird has a story. Record yours.
- For those who hear the rustle.
- Field-ready tools for attentive birders.

### Imagery direction
- Vintage field guide inspiration
- Clean specimen-style layouts
- Earth-toned backgrounds
- Strong whitespace
- Avoid noisy collage aesthetics
- Use species art only when commercially safe and provenance is clear

## Product naming conventions
State series:
- [State] Birding Life List
- [State] Bird Checklist
- [State] Birder Field Notes

Warbler series:
- Warbler Spotting Log
- Warbler Peak Week Tracker
- Warbler Song Codes Field Sheet

Migration series:
- Spring Migration Weekly Log
- First Seen Migration Tracker
- Peak Movement Journal

GoodNotes series:
- Birding Planner for GoodNotes
- Seasonal Birding Planner
- Daily Field Notes Planner

## Pricing posture
Digital singles:
- $4.99–$8.99

Digital bundles:
- $9.99–$19.99

POD later-stage products:
- Price for 70%+ blended gross margin target where possible

Do not underprice to chase volume. Preserve premium utility positioning.

## Listing copy rules
- Front-load product type and search phrase
- Include region/species/theme early
- Make delivery and format explicit
- Avoid keyword stuffing in visible copy
- Always answer: what is it, who is it for, how is it delivered, why is it useful

## Trust and support language
Use:
- Instant digital delivery
- Personal-use birding printable
- Print at home or use on tablet where applicable
- Questions? Contact us and we’ll help

Do not promise:
- Guaranteed bird sightings
- Official ornithological completeness
- Conservation donations until operationally implemented

## Launch merchandising hierarchy

3. Migration trackers
4. GoodNotes planners
5. Humorous giftable birding products later
