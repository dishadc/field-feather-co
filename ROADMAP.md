# Field & Feather Co. Roadmap

Owner: CEO, Hermes
Status: Canonical roadmap document
Last updated: 2026-04-12
Review cadence: Every Monday morning, plus immediate review on trigger events

## 1) The North Star
Field & Feather Co. helps birders build a calmer, richer birding life by pairing trustworthy editorial guidance with refined digital tools that make noticing, recording, and revisiting their field experiences easier. After six months with the product, a customer should feel more confident outdoors, better equipped to identify and remember what they saw, more likely to keep a birding practice alive week after week, and more inclined to treat Field & Feather as both a dependable guide and the place they return for tools, learning, and seasonal momentum.

## 2) Current Sprint (Now)

### 1. Regional cluster session-depth optimization
- Team responsible: Content / SEO / CEO
- One-sentence description: Add stronger related-read architecture and hub journeys across regional birding articles so first-time readers keep moving deeper into the journal.
- User story: As a reader landing on a state guide, I want obvious next articles that match what I am trying to learn, so the journal feels coherent and worth staying in.
- Definition of done: Related-reads modules are added to the regional cluster, internal-link paths are intentional, and QA remains PASS.
- Success metric: Regional articles each expose at least one clear onward path into beginner, ID, or gear content; internal-link density increases without content-integrity regressions.

### 2. Migration cluster session-depth optimization
- Team responsible: Content / SEO / CEO
- One-sentence description: Strengthen links among migration, hawk migration, eBird migration, warbler ID, and shorebird/movement-adjacent pages.
- User story: As a migration-season reader, I want each article to help me decide what to read next, so I can go from general curiosity to practical field confidence quickly.
- Definition of done: Migration cluster related-reads and bridge links are added, and CEO validation remains PASS.
- Success metric: Migration articles each connect users to at least two adjacent use cases (timing, ID, tools, or field behavior).

### 3. Gear cluster trust-and-conversion hardening
- Team responsible: Content / Monetization / CEO
- One-sentence description: Refine gear articles so recommendations remain useful and premium even before full affiliate infrastructure is live.
- User story: As a reader comparing optics, guides, feeders, or apps, I want recommendations that feel polished and trustworthy, so I can act on them without feeling sold to or confused.
- Definition of done: Gear cluster copy is customer-safe, no placeholder/process language remains, and onward journeys from gear into beginner or habitat content are stronger.
- Success metric: Gear pages remain QA-clean and become clearer top-of-funnel trust assets for future monetization.

### 4. Customer-safe subscriber capture foundation
- Team responsible: Growth / Ops / CEO
- One-sentence description: Replace exposed fallback contact/signup mechanics with a branded newsletter architecture that is safe now and ready for Beehiiv later.
- User story: As a customer interested in updates, I want a polished, trustworthy path into the brand’s newsletter, so the company feels real and worth following.
- Definition of done: Personal fallback mailto signup patterns are removed from public storefront surfaces, a branded interim CTA exists, and the roadmap/board reflect the Beehiiv follow-up path.
- Success metric: No exposed fallback mailto/signup patterns remain on public surfaces; QA catches regressions.

## 4) On the Horizon (Next Quarter)

### 1. Editorial-to-commerce flywheel
- Strategic rationale: If the journal reliably turns readers into subscribers and shoppers, Field & Feather becomes more than a content project or product catalog; it becomes a compounding birding brand with lower CAC and stronger trust. This is likely the company’s defining growth engine.
- Open question: Which conversion path performs best — direct product CTA, newsletter-first path, or lead magnet to product journey?

### 2. Permanent publishing and domain cutover
- Strategic rationale: A permanent branded publishing stack will matter for SEO durability, canonical authority, and long-term brand control, but only once it improves trust and workflow rather than adding fragility.
- Open question: When permanent hosting is ready, what migration architecture preserves SEO, canonicals, and editorial momentum without reintroducing public operations leakage?

### 3. Subscriber-led product ecosystem
- Strategic rationale: The strongest version of this company likely combines journal, newsletter, and seasonal digital tools into one recurring birding practice loop. If that works, it increases repeat usage and monetization quality.
- Open question: What recurring value do subscribers want most — seasonal planning, migration notes, curated gear guidance, or regional birding prompts?

### 4. Premium commerce readiness layer
- Strategic rationale: Once revenue blockers clear, the company needs a launch-quality storefront, clean purchase flows, and alignment between editorial demand and purchasable products. This defines whether attention turns into sales.
- Open question: Which product families actually deserve front-of-house prominence once checkout infrastructure is fully live?

## 5) Explicitly Not Building

### 1. A broad generic birding social network
- Why not now: It would dilute focus away from the current editorial + digital tools + commerce flywheel that already has clearer evidence and lower operating cost.
- Condition that changes this decision: We see strong repeated evidence that the audience needs community features to retain value and that such a move would materially improve subscriber or buyer retention.

### 2. Heavy custom app development before the core content-commerce loop works
- Why not now: The company still needs to prove that the journal, subscriber path, and purchasable catalog convert attention into durable value.
- Condition that changes this decision: We identify a repeated user workflow that genuinely requires software rather than content, downloads, or web flows.

### 3. Expanding into broad wildlife or outdoor lifestyle content
- Why not now: The company wins by being unusually specific and trustworthy in birdwatching, not by broadening prematurely into adjacent categories.
- Condition that changes this decision: The birdwatching core becomes clearly dominant and adjacent expansion strengthens, rather than dilutes, the brand.

### 4. Publishing roadmap items with vague labels
- Why not now: Vague roadmap language creates drift, weak delegation, and low trust.
- Condition that changes this decision: Never; if an item cannot be described clearly enough for roadmap structure, it is not ready to be on the roadmap.

## Roadmap review triggers
Update this roadmap immediately if any of the following happen:
- a user insight contradicts a roadmap assumption
- a Now item fails its definition of done
- a major competitor or platform changes the landscape
- growth or conversion signals shift materially
- a blocker reveals sequencing is wrong
- the CEO changes their mind about priority for any reason

## Weekly roadmap ritual
Every Monday morning, before major execution begins:
1. Read the North Star
2. Audit Now
3. Review Up Next
4. Reassess On the Horizon
5. Send a short roadmap brief to affected leads/workstreams

## Monthly roadmap session
Once a month, run a focused roadmap review to answer:
- Is the North Star still true?
- Are Up Next items truly the highest-leverage next moves?
- What are we building because we still believe in it, versus because it is already written down?
- What one roadmap bet changes everything if it works?
- What one bet are we least confident in, and what evidence would improve confidence?
